Obtaining or developing knowledge, behaviors or skills in a learning environment on the Internet

Stage 2

Stage 3

MOOC (Massive Open Online Course): a new way of learning in a networked world, based on the
Theory of Connectivism

Principles of Connectivism

  • Learning is a process of connecting information sources
  • The ability to see connections between fields, ideas, and concepts is a core skill

Principles of Connectivism

  • Knowing where to find information is more important than knowing information
  • Learning happens in different ways: courses, communities, conversations, web search, email lists, blogs, etc.

Principles of Connectivism

  • Choosing what to learn is itself a learning process
  • Learning is a knowledge creation process, not only knowledge consumption

Principles of MOOCs

1. Content is not prepared ahead of time, but is an aggregation of a massive amount of content produced in different places

Principles of MOOCs

2. Remixing materials created within the course with each other and with materials elsewhere

Principles of MOOCs

3. Re-purposing of aggregated and remixed materials to suit goals of each participant

Principles of MOOCs

4. Feeding forward by sharing re-purposed ideas and content with other participants and the rest of the world

MOOCs - Some Analogies

  • There are more football games in the world than you could possibly watch
  • There are more food recipes in the world than you could experience in a lifetime

Benefits of MOOCs

  • Free for all who are interested
  • Learning occurs in an informal manner rather than in a classroom
  • All work, thoughts and instruction can be shared by all participants

Benefits of MOOCs

  • You choose what you do
  • You choose how to participate
  • You decide if you have been successful

Assurance - Formal Methods

Mathematical proofs to ensure that the behavior of the code follows the behavior of the specification.

Assurance - Formal Methods

Used only when the cost to repair errors is very high, the features are very hard to test, and the item being verified is not too large.

Assurance - Formal Methods

Examples include vital parts of hardware (like network protocols) and safety critical software systems (like medical equipment).

Service Oriented Architecture

A software architecture where all components are designed to be services.

Service Oriented Architecture

Components of an application act as interoperable services, and can be used independently and recombined in other applications.
